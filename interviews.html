Interviews

Overview of Interviews

Job interviews are one of the most crucial steps in your career search process. It’s the organization’s opportunity to determine mutual fit. This guide provides up-to-date best practices and step-by-step instructions for mastering the conversation. There are three critical questions, often referred to as “the big three,” that you need to be able to address in preparation:

  • Why You: articulating a response to "talk about yourself"
  • Why This Position: understanding the role demands through job description and online research
  • Why This Organization: understanding this organization and the industry.

The Interview Process

  • Introduction: U.S. employers value confident and assertive behavior assessed through professional attire, eye contact, and body language. “Tell me about yourself” often serves as the opening question and sets the tone for the rest of the conversation — this is your opportunity to build rapport and likability with your interviewers. A strong, focused response can help you make a positive first impression and guide the interview in a direction that highlights your strengths.
  • Interviewer background: Understand the job, the organization, and where you fit. Review and dissect the job description to know how your qualifications can benefit the employer and match them to the job description.
  • Discussion (Q&A):Be prepared to articulate your qualifications and back it up with specific examples. One of the best ways to prepare for interview questions is to review the STAR technique, as behavioral interview questions are used in all rounds of interviews.
  • Questions you want to ask: It's important not to overlook this area when preparing for your interview. It's your opportunity to demonstrate your genuine interest in the employer and the job. Furthermore, feel empowered to ask thoughtful querstions that will help you determine if this is somewhere you'd like to work.
  • Thank you and follow up: Send thank-you emails to your interviewers within 24 hours following the interview. If a panel interviews you, send separate emails. Your thank-you note should also include a reference to one key exchange in the interview and once again convey interest in the position and commitment to contribute to the organization's success.

Before the Interview: Positioning and Additional Resources

Research is essential before any interview because it helps you position yourself in terms of the employer’s needs — not just what you want from the job. Understanding the organization’s mission, values, recent developments, and the role allows you to tailor your responses and show how your background aligns with their goals. It also helps you ask thoughtful questions and demonstrate genuine interest. Be sure to review the company’s website, news updates, job description, and, if possible, information about your interviewers.
